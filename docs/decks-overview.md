# Decks — why they exist

Each deck was created to solve a specific real problem, not to "study English in general."

---

## it-deck
**Why:** Work communication — tickets, standups, code review, architecture discussions.
Knowing individual terms wasn't enough; couldn't construct fluent sentences under pressure.

**Approach:** Each word taught through 5-7 sentence examples across different tenses and
contexts. Heavy focus on how the word behaves in a sentence, not just what it means.

**Gap being filled:** Active production — taking a Ukrainian thought and saying it naturally
in English in a work context.

---

## video-by-movies
**Why:** Ear training and natural spoken English. Books and courses teach written language;
real people speak differently.

**Content selection principle:** Only shows watched multiple times in translation — context
is already known, so 100% focus goes to the English itself (comprehensible input).

**Current content:**
- Sherlock (BBC) — S1 complete. 660 clips. British English, complex sentence structure,
  sharp precise phrasing. Fast monologues (deduction scenes) skipped — too dense to clip.
- Silicon Valley (HBO) — S1 ~half done, want to finish. 343 clips. American tech/startup
  English, office slang, natural IT context.
- The Secret Life of Pets — complete. 273 clips. Conversational American English,
  simpler sentences, good for listening fluency.

## l2-vocab
**Why:** Attending ESOL L2 Writing/Reading courses. New vocabulary comes up in class —
cards lock it in before it disappears.

**Approach:** Reactive — word appears in course material → card created same day or soon after.

---

## medicine
**Why:** Can't explain symptoms or needs to a GP in English. Even basic medical
conversations were a struggle.

**Gap being filled:** Practical survival vocabulary — describing symptoms, understanding
diagnoses, asking the right questions at appointments.

---

## interview
**Why:** Started doing mock interviews and immediately hit a wall — knew individual terms
but couldn't construct sentences under pressure. Stu

**Core problem:** Terms exist in passive memory but won't come out as fluent speech when
stressed and timed. The gap is between "I know this word" and "I can use it right now."

**Note:** This is exactly what it-deck is designed to fix at a deeper level — sentence
production, not word recognition. interview deck captures specific pain points observed
in mock sessions.

---

## book
**Why:** Reading English books — interesting phrases, expressions, or constructions worth
keeping. More literary/written register than other decks.

**Approach:** Opportunistic — no target list, just things that stand out while reading.
